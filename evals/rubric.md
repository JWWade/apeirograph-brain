# Eval Rubric

Use this lightweight rubric for manual review and prompt comparison.

## Dimensions

### Correctness
- Are the notes, chord qualities, and tonal claims musically sound?
- Does the answer avoid invented harmonic facts?

### Usefulness
- Does the output help a musician make sense of the object or choose a next move?
- Are the structured fields or candidates actionable?

### Clarity
- Is the wording concise, readable, and direct?
- Is the reasoning easy to review quickly?

## Suggested scoring

Use a simple 1 to 5 scale:

- 5 = strong
- 4 = solid with minor issues
- 3 = mixed
- 2 = weak
- 1 = unusable

## Comparison workflow

1. Run the eval summary.
2. Review the per-case previews.
3. Compare average scores across prompt or model changes.
